Couscous

Description

Couscous is a Berber dish of small (about 3 millimetres (0.12 in) diameter) steamed balls of crushed durum wheat semolina that is traditionally served with a stew spooned on top. Pearl millet and sorghum, especially in the Sahel, and other cereals can be cooked in a similar way and the resulting dishes are also sometimes called couscous.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves

Steps

  • Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook and stir onion and garlic in hot oil until lightly browned, 7 to 10 minutes.
  • Stir cumin, turmeric, coriander, cinnamon, ginger, paprika, cayenne pepper, cardamom, cloves, nutmeg, salt, and black pepper into the onion mixture; cook and stir until fragrant, 2 to 3 minutes.
  • Mix chicken, carrots, zucchini, garbanzo beans, chicken broth, tomatoes, raisins, apricots, honey, and lemon juice into spice mixture; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover the skillet, and simmer until the chicken is no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about 30 minutes.
  • Whisk cornstarch and water together in a small bowl until the cornstarch dissolves; stir into the stew. Simmer until the stew has thickened, about 5 minutes. Garnish with cilantro to serve.
  • Enjoy!

Bastilla

Description

Bastilla is a pie of Andalusian origin eaten in Morocco and Algeria. It is a sweet and salty pie, made with a dough called "warqa" or "ouarka" (a very thin dough), stuffed with a mixture of chicken, almonds and eggs. It is usually served as a starter or as a main course.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast halves, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup chopped zucchini
  • 1 (15 ounce) can garbanzo beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup chopped dried apricots
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook and stir onion and garlic in hot oil until lightly browned, 7 to 10 minutes.
  2. Stir cumin, turmeric, coriander, cinnamon, ginger, paprika, cayenne pepper, cardamom, cloves, nutmeg, salt, and black pepper into the onion mixture; cook and stir until fragrant, 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Mix chicken, carrots, zucchini, garbanzo beans, chicken broth, tomatoes, raisins, apricots, honey, and lemon juice into spice mixture; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover the skillet, and simmer until the chicken is no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about 30 minutes.
  4. Whisk cornstarch and water together in a small bowl until the cornstarch dissolves; stir into the stew. Simmer until the stew has thickened, about 5 minutes. Garnish with cilantro to serve.
  5. Enjoy!
